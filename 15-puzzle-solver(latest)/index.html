Fun Facts

The 15-puzzle was invented in the 1870s by Noyes Palmer Chapman, a postmaster in Canastota, New York.

In 1880, Sam Loyd falsely claimed to have invented the puzzle and offered a $1,000 prize for a solution to an impossible configuration.

Only half of all possible 15-puzzle configurations are solvable. The other half are mathematically impossible!

The 15-puzzle has been the subject of mathematical research in group theory and is related to permutation groups.

Puzzle Tips

Start with Corners

Begin by placing the corner pieces (1, 4, 13) in their correct positions. This creates a foundation for solving the rest.

Row by Row

Solve the puzzle row by row, starting from the top. Once a row is complete, avoid moving those pieces again.

Last Two Rows

The last two rows are tricky! Solve them as a single unit using circular movements to position tiles correctly.

Keyboard Shortcuts

Use 'R' to reset, 'S' to shuffle, and 'A' to auto-solve the puzzle!
